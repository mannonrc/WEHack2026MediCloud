## MediCloud

MediCloud is a proposal for a joint application and application programming interface (API) for clinicians and patients to have access to medical history and data anytime, anywhere.Data fragmentation in clinical settings, such as lack of access to comprehensive medical history, inability to integrate data provided by wearable devices, or split measurements in clinical surveillance systems, can act as an obstacle in a physician's path to making a correct and thorough diagnosis or therapeutic decision. By combining data from multiple practitioners and consultations, a patient's wearable devices, and clinical surveillance systems if available, consultations and treatments can be conducted more efficiently and holistically, considering a patient's past and current states of health. 

This project was designed as a response to two challenge statements at the University of Texas at Dallas' Women Engineers Who Hack (WEHack) competition in April 2026 over a 24 hour period. These challenges are listed below. The project's application frontend was conceptualized in Figma and can be explored further in the directories "MediCloudPatientDash" and "MediCloudPhysicianDash". The physician, Dr. Meredith Grey, and patient, Astronaut Christina Koch, are simply examples and inspiration for characters, not real people nor accurate medical information. The API's backend was also designed in Figma and can be found in the corresponding file "APIBackendNetworkDiagram".
